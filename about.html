About Rainydays

Founder Jo Molver

Our history

It all began in a beautiful forest in Norway, where 18-year old Jo Molver hiked with his grandfather. The rain was pouring, they were drenched through despite wearing rainwear, and Jo complained to his grandfather that every rain jacket he had ever owned did not keep its promise of keeping him dry, and his grandfather said; “Then why don’t you make a better one?”, and so following his idol’s suggestion, that is what Jo set out to do.

In the following years, Jo and his growing team focused all their energy into making affordable, sustainable and comfortable clothing for active people, and now Rainydays has sold more than 160 000 rain jackets to happy, tenacious adventurers.

Our mission and vision

Being active and exploring what the nature has to offer is core to our happiness and health, and so clothes and high prices shouldn’t stop us from doing what we love.

"We make our clothes durable with high quality for an affordable price, so that as many people as possible can enjoy the fresh air no matter the weather."" - Jo Molver

We and our customers want to spend as much time outside as possible, and so it is important for us to preserve our nature as best we can, limiting the ecological impact. Therefore, we make clothes that last long, using organic cotton and recyclable materials, and donate 1% of our income to environmental groups.

Our team

Our team is carefully chosen, and consists of people consistent with our target group; active and adventurous. A requirement for joining our team is to be environmentally conscious, as Rainydays focuses on sustainability.
